  • Log Analytics Service

LogLogic Unity is the event processing application for machine data. Log data isn't just for security anymore. Log and machine data is being harnessed to provide insight into IT operational efficiencies. As the visual designer for this project, I have been working closely with IXD to accomplish a great solution for this project. We also set up a good working relationship and process with product team in order to achieve better efficiencies. The visual pattern is the key to make everything goes smoothly and benefits the entire team.

1.0 design

The design of 1.0 has been done in a very tight deadline. The goal is more about feature complete and refresh the UI. So at that point, form PM to design team spent more time on the interaction and work flow, on the visual design, I tried to make things as fresh as possible. And the UI has been redesigned in 2.0 after the 1.0 release, I worked closely with PM and UX team to re-think about the color pallete, making sure all the products look consistent in its vertical.
The another important redesign is the spacing optimization, the 2.0 has reduced a lot more unused spaces and brought more real estate to the content.

Color Study

2.0 design

In the design 2.0, I have worked closely with PM to figure out the color for the product. Since it is belongs to the Operational group, I neeed to make sure all proudcts look consistent in the group. In 1.0. the flaw is the color was all over the place, in 2.0, I tried to nail down the primary color into 2-3 colors, and it looks way stronger than before in terms of the branding.

UI Pattern

Although the design has been changed quite a lot, but I still documenting the UI patterns through out the process, such as table style, dropdown, checkbox, etc. So the compoments still stays the same thing while its updating. It saves a lot of time for the current and future re-design process.

"Try It On" App
Instructor: Greiman, April&Slote, Bob
” Try It On ” – Inspired by ” Purikura ”
In Japan, ” Purikura ” is the moniker of a special type of photo booths that produce photo stickers. Still maintaining huge popularity for girls in Japan, it has spread throughout Australia and East Asia to Taiwan,South Korea, Hong Kong, Philippines, China, Vietnam, and Thailand. To have fun with this contraption , girls get together and take a funny expression photo in the photo sticker machine, then they add some funny graphics around the images by the machines or hand drawing. After applying finishing touches, they share pictures to each other as interesting memories.

  • Cubic Distort in Tokyo

Cubic Distort in Tokyo
Software: Photoshop, C4D
Instructor: Michael Brunsfeld
1)I created a sequence of cubes in C4D and rendered out for the Photoshop. I used ” Liquefy ” tool and tweaked the alpha channels of the images of cubes. The goal is to create some abstract shapes to match the serene photo and to form an unconventionally intriguing style.
2)Created a montage by fracturing a scene into many pieces. Select and cut pieces and jump them to a new layer —then ALTER those pieces. Tried distorting them, re-coloring them, overlapping them, re-combining them, changing opacities, then adding drop shadows to separate and get things to PoP!
