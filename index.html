"Try It On" App
Instructor: Greiman, April&Slote, Bob
” Try It On ” – Inspired by ” Purikura ”
In Japan, ” Purikura ” is the moniker of a special type of photo booths that produce photo stickers. Still maintaining huge popularity for girls in Japan, it has spread throughout Australia and East Asia to Taiwan,South Korea, Hong Kong, Philippines, China, Vietnam, and Thailand. To have fun with this contraption , girls get together and take a funny expression photo in the photo sticker machine, then they add some funny graphics around the images by the machines or hand drawing. After applying finishing touches, they share pictures to each other as interesting memories.
I was the UX/UI designer for theis project, I spent hours to gather feedbacks from the target audience to make sure this concept is implementable, so I did serveral rounds of user interview, 90% of user are really interested about this app. The deisgn was shown as a clickable prototype, after several rounds of user testings, the final design was exactly finished with user's expectation.

Cubic Distortion(Tokyo)

Experimenting Photoshop with Cinema 4D to create an intriguing digital imaging.
1)I created a sequence of cubes in C4D and rendered out for the Photoshop. I used ” Liquefy ” tool and tweaked the alpha channels of the images of cubes. The goal is to create some abstract shapes to match the serene photo and to form an unconventionally intriguing style.
2)Created a montage by fracturing a scene into many pieces. Select and cut pieces and jump them to a new layer —then ALTER those pieces. Tried distorting them, re-coloring them, overlapping them, re-combining them, changing opacities, then adding drop shadows to separate and get things to PoP!
